Falconets fight back to draw China to reach quarterfinals

2018 FIFA U-20 Women's World Cup Falconets fight back to draw China to reach quarterfinals

Ajibade's 95th-minute goal gave the Falconets one point needed to progress from Group D.

  • Steve Dede
  • Print
  • eMail
Tweet

news

The Falconets of Nigeria fought back to draw China 1-1 in their last Group D game of the 2018 FIFA U-20 Women's World Cup to reach the quarter-finals.

After losing one game and winning the other, Nigeria's U-20 Women needed to avoid a loss for a spot in the quarterfinals.

But it was the Chinese who took the lead through Linyan Zhang who netted in the 41st minute.

play China took the lead in the first half (Getty Images)

The diminutive Zhang turned her Nigerian marker in the box before firing past Falconets goalkeeper Chiamaka Nnadozie to give the Asians the lead just four minutes before the break.

With that scoreline, China were on their way to the quarterfinals and knowing that they were on their home, the Falconets pushed hard in search of an equaliser in the second half.

As the Falconets pushed, China goalkeeper Huan Xu was proving to be a stumbling block as she pulled off a couple of saves.

She had little, however, to do as Rasheedat Ajibade turned home Aishat Bello’s cross at the near post in stoppage time to spark huge celebrations from the Nigerian bench.
